About

An institutional approach
to UAE real estate.

Duneshore Capital is a real estate private equity business being established in the United Arab Emirates. We identify, structure, and manage real estate investments across the United Arab Emirates — partnering with strong operators and platforms to generate institutional-quality returns.

Our approach is rooted in rigorous investment discipline, institutional governance, and genuine alignment with our investors. We take a long-term view on capital, relationships, and value creation.

We are currently in formation. For further information about Duneshore Capital, please get in touch directly.

Approach

How we invest.

01

Freehold Assets in UAE Growth Markets

We focus primarily on freehold zones across the UAE — areas where corporate entities can hold direct ownership rights. Freehold structures provide the clearest path to capital appreciation and exit, and are well-suited to institutional fund frameworks.

02

Backing Strong Operators & Platforms

We partner with proven real estate operators, JV partners, and platforms — providing institutional capital, fund structure, and governance alongside their execution capability. We back people, not just assets.

03

Institutional Governance from Day One

Every investment is structured with institutional-grade documentation, governance, and reporting from the outset. We apply the same standards to every transaction that institutional investors expect from established fund managers.

Founder

Neil Sarkhel

Neil Sarkhel is a real estate fund manager, capital raiser, and business builder with 16 years of institutional real estate private equity experience.

Over ten years at Newcore Capital, he helped build a UK real estate fund management business to $800m of assets under management — structuring seven institutional funds, raising $600m+ of capital, and managing 120+ investor relationships across pension funds, insurance companies, family offices, and high-net-worth individuals. He exited his ownership stake in August 2025.

Neil holds a Masters in Real Estate Finance from the University of Cambridge, attended the Oxford Real Estate Programme at Saïd Business School, and began his career at Goldman Sachs in London. He is based in Dubai.
